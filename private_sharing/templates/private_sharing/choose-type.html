How will your participants be consented and connect to Open Humans?

There are two options. Your choice here affects the technical process for members consenting to your study and opting to share data with you!

Give some thought as to what data, information, and permissions you'll request access to through Open Humans. After activity or study approval, this list may no longer be edited.

A. "OAuth2"

Informed consent occurs on your study website. Users create accounts on your site, data access occurs through OAuth2 authorization. (Note: you can use the OAuth2 data you access to perform “social authentication” as the basis of user accounts on your site.)

Overview

Your participants consent to your study elsewhere, outside Open Humans.

  1. You provide Open Humans with your study’s URL for enrollment—we’ll share this with members interested in contributing to your study.
  2. Participants go to your website to perform informed consent and enroll.
  3. You’ll use an OAuth2 connection to request access to their Open Humans data.

Create an OAuth2 data request activity

B. Informed consent occurs on Open Humans

  1. You provide us with text consent to present to members.
  2. Members are presented with this consent. If they agree, they’ll be presented with the data sharing request. This should look the same as the data sharing request presenting for process A.
