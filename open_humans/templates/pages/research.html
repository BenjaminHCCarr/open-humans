Public data API

Get started right away with data sets some Open Humans members have generously chosen to share publicly!

Data is publicly listed on members' public profiles, and is also available through our Public Data API. It's very easy to use – because the API only returns public data, no authentication is required!

Users of the API are expected to follow our guidelines for using public data.

Work with our members

Interested in more than the public data? You can easily get data directly from members, and to enable additional follow-up research (e.g. surveys or new samples). Open Humans is an open cohort for you to work with!

Benefits for your study include:

  • API access to private data members choose to share directly with you
  • working with a motivated community of members
  • ability to perform additional research with members
  • see your study featured by Open Humans

Create a project on our website and you'll immediately be able to work with up to 10 members. You can get started immediately! Once you're done drafting and testing, you'll need to contact us for approval to work with more members. Once approved, we can share your project with our members.

Let us help you

Governance and technology to enable data sharing can be a challenge! We have experience and expertise that can help you do this.

Ask us for advice at support@openhumans.org

Connect your study

Open Humans believes researchers that work with our members should also be committed to building the commons: we want to see research partners to connect their studies and return data to participants. By doing this, research partners commit to transparency and equity, and their participants can use and share the resulting data.

We want to support researchers interested in giving raw data access to their participants. Reasons you might want to do this include:

  • see your study listed on Open Humans
  • make your participants valued partners in research
  • pursue new methods of data sharing
  • create a path to public data, shared by some participants
  • see your work cited when other studies use your data
Ask us about connecting studies at support@openhumans.org
