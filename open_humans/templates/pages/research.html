Ways to work with us

Work with our members

We're also interested in enabling studies to work directly with Open Humans members, to receive data (private as well as public) and to enable additional follow-up research (e.g. surveys or new samples). Benefits for your study include:

  • working with a motivated community of members
  • access to private data members choose to share with you
  • ability to perform additional research with members
  • see your study featured by Open Humans

Open Humans believes researchers that work with our members should also be committed to building the commons: we expect research partners to connect their studies and return data to participants. By doing this, research partners commit to transparency and equity, and their participants can use and share the resulting data.

We're also available to assist you. We have lots of experience with conducting studies online, including: building websites, online informed consent, and APIs for integrating with Open Humans. Consider engaging us to build the informatics and operations needed to make your study a success!

Let us help you

Governance and technology to enable data sharing can be a challenge! We have experience and expertise that can help you do this.

Contact us about using our data sharing expertise

Connect your study

We want to support researchers interested in giving raw data access to their participants. Reasons you might want to do this include:

  • see your study listed on Open Humans
  • make your participants valued partners in research
  • pursue new methods of data sharing
  • create a path to public data, shared by some participants
  • see your work cited when other studies use your data
Contact us about connecting your study

Use public data

You can get started right away with public data on Open Humans.

Some Open Humans members generously choose to publicly share their data. We hope this data can be a public resource for research, education, and other diverse purposes. If you're interested in using this data, please be sure to read the Open Humans' guidelines for using public data.
