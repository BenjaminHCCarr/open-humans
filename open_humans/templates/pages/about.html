About Open Humans

Your data is more powerful when you're a part of it. Open Humans combines community and technology to help people access their data, explore it, study it, and donate it. When you're connected to your data, you have a potential for great good: researchers can ask you new questions, and can combine data from disparate data sources. Through you, they can do research that was otherwise impossible.

You decide when to share. The data members collect is always private by default. Members can choose which projects to share their data with – to explore data analysis tools, or contribute it to a good cause. Some members even decide to make some of their data sources a public resource! We celebrate sharing, but the decision is yours: you choose what to share, and who to share it with.

We are all part of discovery. We believe this in a very fundamental sense! We can all contribute our data to research, but Open Humans offers more: anyone can create a project. This means a patient community can create a "data commons" to combine its data, and bring it to research teams themselves. About Open Humans Foundation

Open Humans is a program of the 501(c)(3) nonprofit Open Humans Foundation and has been funded by the Robert Wood Johnson Foundation, Knight Foundation, and Shuttleworth Foundation. Our 2015 launch was written up in Forbes, Newsweek, Scientific American, and more.

Website policies

We have a very readable Terms of Use, Data Use Policy, and Community Guidelines. We welcome feedback on these documents publicly or privately.

Open Source

We believe in sharing. This website is an open source project, as are as other modules, packages, and code in our technical stack. You're welcome to create "issues" in our repository and comment on our code. However, please report security issues privately by emailing us at security@openhumans.org.

Questions?

If you have any questions for us, feel free to contact us, or join the Open Humans Community Slack to chat with us and other members of the community.

Staff

In a sense, our "team" is enormous – the Open Humans community is filled with amazing members, citizen scientists, and researchers that make science something we can all be part of. We're humbled by this! Our formal staff is a small team committed to making this community a success.
