Enrolling in research partner studies

Each of our research partner studies have their own enrollment process, with different protocols and requirements. Research partner studies generally require participants to enroll themselves and be at least 18 years of age.

Note: Many of the buttons below will take you to a different website. You might want to open these links in a new tab.

Here’s our current list:

  • American Gut

    Who lives on your skin - and in your gut? Take samples to test your microbiome!

    Notes

    • All participants that return kits are expected to get data!
    • To receive a kit and enroll, a donation of $99 is usually required.
    • To connect your participant account you’ll need to have received a kit.

    Enroll through American Gut's website

  • GoViral

    Collect samples when you get sick for viral testing. Find out what virus actually got you sick!

    Notes

    • You can enroll online and connect your account immediately.
    • Participation is free.
    • Not all participants will generate data - because not everyone gets sick! Once you have a kit, only 20% of individuals get sick enough to submit a sample in any given season.
    • Kits might be distributed only to a subset of participants. For example, in the 2014-2015 flu season kits were distributed only to Massachusetts and New York residents – this was to concentrate on more densely sampling particular geographic regions.

    Enroll through GoViral's website

  • Harvard Personal Genome Project

    How does our genome influence our health and traits? Donate a sample to have you genome sequenced and shared as a public resource.

    Notes

    • You can enroll online and connect your account immediately.
    • Participation is free.
    • Participants must be willing to make their genomic and other data publicly shared. These data are potentially identifiable and anonymity is not guaranteed.
    • Genome sequencing is currently expensive. Sample collections and sequencing are done as resources are available. Participants are not guaranteed to be sequenced, and sequencing may take years to occur.

    Enroll through PGP Harvard's website

Our site also has one ongoing study associated which allows you to publicly share data on your profile!

  • Public Data Sharing

    Want to publicly share data on your Open Humans profile? Enroll in our study!

    You'll immediately get a badge for your profile when you enroll in this study – no additional connections necessary!

    Notes

    • You can enroll online immediately.
    • Participation is free.
    • Unlocks the ability to publicly share data on your profile.
    • No additional account connections necessary (this is part of the Open Humans website).
    • All public sharing is "opt in": you select which files to share.
    • Even if you have data publicly shared through another study (e.g. PGP Harvard) these data will not be public on Open Humans unless you choose to make it public here.

    Enroll in Public Data Sharing
